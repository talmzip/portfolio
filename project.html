Hemoglobin Class

A VR biology lesson designed to help students understand the structure and function of hemoglobin through face-to-face encounter. Designed for VR first-timers with fun and intuitive interactions & guidance. Deployed on +500 students.

The tutorial - starting from the very beginning.
Prof David Shprinruk, me, and students in a pilot.

Designed to be operated on 10 VR first-timers simultaneously, by a single operator.

I knew it had to be 100% self-guided, with intuitive and inviting interactions.

Students described it as a learning environment that invites "aha" moments.

I discovered the power of learning by seeing familiar ideas from a new perspective.

Selection-Question Interface.
Pointing-Question Interface.

I wasn't sure VR was the right medium, so we built quick, simple prototypes to test its value.

One placed hemoglobin models in a virtual room, and the immediate excitement from lecturers and students proved its impact.
