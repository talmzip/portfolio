XR In Science, An Experience

Explaining the power of XR in science through direct, first-hand experience with the ideas of behavioral data tracking and embodiment.

Seven mini experiences that turn abstract ideas into tangible, emotional encounters. A few examples:

The eye-tracking museum

In a virtual museum, after a few minutes of exploration, a real-time heat map of users’ gaze appeared on the walls, floor, and ceiling, revealing the precision and intrusiveness of eye tracking.

The movement-tracking maze

After navigating a maze to find a hidden coin, users returned to a miniature version of it, seeing a tiny avatar retracing their exact path and body language.

Calibrating the physical to the virtual

We recreated the physical room in VR to ease new users into the experience and reduce disorientation. I call it “Fake Passthrough”.
